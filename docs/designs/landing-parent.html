the moment

your 4-year-old is on the kitchen floor because you said no to a third popsicle. screaming, kicking, you can feel yourself about to snap.

try saying

"i can see you really wanted that popsicle. you can be mad. the answer is still no."

— from the cheat sheet "validate the want, hold the limit" · PCIT

1get low. eye level. breathe with them for 10 seconds.
2name the want: "you really wanted that popsicle."
3hold the limit warmly. no negotiation.
4offer two acceptable alternatives when they're ready.

ParentScript unlocks skills session by session — only what your therapist has approved. nothing else. no in-app store. no algorithm.

scripts your therapist stands behind

every word cites the framework it came from. PCIT, BPT, CPS, Triple P. nothing made up. nothing paraphrased.

practice logs that don't shame

tap a button to log what you tried. your therapist sees a real picture — not just what you remember on thursday.

install it on your phone, not just your laptop

works on ios and android. opens fast. the moment you reach for it is the moment it's already loaded.

your therapist controls the pace

skills unlock when your therapist unlocks them. no more, no less. if something isn't working, they lock it back down.

no identifying info required

children are stored with names you choose. no last names, no birthdates, no school names. we don't want what we don't need.

between sessions, not alone

the app reminds you what your therapist said last time. you don't have to remember everything. that's the point.

if it gets too big, here's where to call.

ParentScript is a coaching tool, not a crisis tool. if your child is in immediate danger, if they're talking about hurting themselves, if the moment is bigger than what an app can hold — these numbers are always one tap away.

when the app senses crisis-class language, it surfaces these numbers automatically. no LLM is allowed to "respond" to that moment.
