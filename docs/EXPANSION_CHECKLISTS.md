## 1. ANXIETY DISORDERS

### Therapist Onboarding Checklist

- [ ] Verify state licensure and malpractice coverage
- [ ] Complete MAZE anxiety module certification (2-hour asynchronous + 1-hour live Q&A)
- [ ] Review GAD-7 administration protocol and scoring cutoffs
- [ ] Confirm familiarity with exposure hierarchy construction
- [ ] Set up emergency contact and after-hours escalation pathway
- [ ] Review app's safety escalation flow (988 → therapist → 911)
- [ ] Sign module-specific consent acknowledging limits of app-based care
- [ ] Complete 2 practice runs of In-the-Moment coaching script with test client

### Patient Intake Checklist

- [ ] Administer GAD-7 + PHQ-9 (comorbidity screen)
- [ ] Identify primary anxiety subtype (GAD, social, panic, phobia, OCD)
- [ ] Map avoidance patterns and safety behaviors
- [ ] Assess for substance use as self-medication
- [ ] Screen for suicidal ideation (C-SSRS if PHQ-9 item 9 > 0)
- [ ] Collect trauma history (ACEs brief, if relevant)
- [ ] Identify family anxiety history and accommodation patterns
- [ ] Set baseline SUDS anchors (0 = calm, 100 = worst panic)
- [ ] Establish therapy goals in patient's own words
- [ ] Provide app onboarding (install PWA, walk through In-the-Moment button)

### Session-by-Session Skill Progression (L1–L5)

| Level             | Session Range | Skills Unlocked                                                                                                  | Mastery Checkpoint                                                                        |
| ----------------- | ------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **L1 Foundation** | 1–2           | Psychoeducation (anxiety cycle), diaphragmatic breathing, grounding (5-4-3-2-1)                                  | Patient can explain fight-flight-freeze in own words                                      |
| **L2 Awareness**  | 3–4           | Thought record intro, cognitive distortions (catastrophizing, mind-reading), worry time scheduling               | Completes 3 thought records between sessions                                              |
| **L3 Skills**     | 5–8           | Exposure hierarchy building, in-vivo exposure (least feared), response prevention basics, behavioral experiments | Completes 2 exposures with SUDS pre/post tracking                                         |
| **L4 Deep Work**  | 9–12          | Intermediate exposures, interoceptive exposure (panic), ACT defusion, acceptance practice                        | SUDS peak drops 30+ points from first exposure; patient tolerates distress without escape |
| **L5 Mastery**    | 13–16         | Complex/highest exposures, relapse prevention plan, values-based action despite anxiety, self-compassion routine | Maintains gains 2+ weeks without session; has written relapse plan                        |

### In-the-Moment Coaching Scripts (3-Step Crisis)

**Panic Attack Script**

1. **GROUND** → "Name 5 things you see. 4 you hear. 3 you can touch. 2 you smell. 1 you taste."
2. **BREATHE** → "Inhale 4 counts. Hold 4. Exhale 6. Repeat 3 times. Your body is having a false alarm."
3. **RIDE** → "This will pass in 10–20 minutes. It has before. Stay where you are if safe. Do not flee."

**Social Anxiety Script**

1. **NOTICE** → "Your mind is predicting rejection. That's a thought, not a fact."
2. **DROP ANCHOR** → "Feel your feet on the floor. Straighten your posture. One slow breath."
3. **ACT** → "You don't need to feel calm to enter. Walk in. Say one word. That's enough."

**OCD Intrusive Thought Script**

1. **LABEL** → "That's an intrusive thought. Not a command. Not a prediction."
2. **ALLOW** → "Don't push it away. Don't analyze it. Let it sit there like background noise."
3. **REDIRECT** → "Turn toward what you value. Do one small action aligned with that value right now."

### Safety Escalation Protocols

| Trigger                                                   | Immediate Action                                                | Escalation Path                                                                |
| --------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| GAD-7 item 8 (fear of dying) + panic symptoms             | Coach grounding + breathing; assess for cardiac symptoms        | If chest pain + first episode → 911; otherwise therapist callback within 2 hrs |
| PHQ-9 item 9 > 0                                          | Surface C-SSRS in app; block non-crisis content until completed | C-SSRS positive → 988 + therapist alert + safety plan review                   |
| Exposure SUDS > 80 and patient requests stop              | Validate distress; do not force continuation; offer grounding   | Therapist notification; review hierarchy grading at next session               |
| Patient using app > 15x/day (reassurance-seeking pattern) | Flag in dashboard; suggest therapist review                     | Therapist coaching on tolerance of uncertainty                                 |

### Progress Tracking Metrics

- GAD-7 total score (target: drop ≥ 5 points by session 8)
- SUDS peak per exposure session (target: 30+ point reduction from first to final)
- Weekly practice log count (target: ≥ 4 entries/week)
- Thought record completion rate (target: ≥ 70% of assigned)
- Safety behavior reduction (therapist-rated 0–10 scale)
- Functional impairment score (target: return to work/school/social baseline)

### Family/Caregiver Involvement

- **Psychoeducation session** (session 1–2): family learns anxiety cycle, role of accommodation
- **Accommodation reduction plan**: family identifies 3 accommodations (reassurance, avoidance, ritual participation); therapist sets graded reduction targets
- **Family exposure support**: coached on how to encourage vs. rescue during patient distress
- **Parent/caregiver GAD-7**: screen caregiver anxiety; high scores may compromise patient progress
- **Weekly family check-in**: 10-minute structured update via app (what worked, what was hard, questions for therapist)

## 2. ADDICTION / SUBSTANCE USE

### Therapist Onboarding Checklist

- [ ] Verify licensure includes SUD scope (or partner with ASAM-certified provider)
- [ ] Complete MAZE addiction module training (3 hours: MI basics, stages of change, trigger mapping)
- [ ] Review AUDIT/DAST administration and scoring
- [ ] Confirm MAT knowledge or referral pathway (buprenorphine, naltrexone, acamprosate)
- [ ] Set up 24-hour crisis protocol (overdose response, naloxone locator)
- [ ] Review harm-reduction philosophy vs. abstinence-only stance
- [ ] Sign module consent acknowledging medical complexity
- [ ] Complete 2 role-plays: motivational interviewing + craving surfacing

### Patient Intake Checklist

- [ ] Administer AUDIT (alcohol) + DAST-10 (drugs)
- [ ] Assess primary substance(s), route, frequency, quantity, last use
- [ ] Screen for withdrawal risk (CIWA-Ar if alcohol; COWS if opioids)
- [ ] Medical history: liver function, cardiac, pregnancy, psychiatric meds
- [ ] Suicide screen (C-SSRS) — SUD elevates risk significantly
- [ ] Assess stage of change (precontemplation → contemplation → preparation → action)
- [ ] Map triggers: people, places, emotions, times, events
- [ ] Identify social support network and living environment stability
- [ ] Assess legal/occupational consequences and motivation drivers
- [ ] Establish baseline use diary (7-day retrospective)

### Session-by-Session Skill Progression (L1–L5)

| Level             | Session Range | Skills Unlocked                                                                                                            | Mastery Checkpoint                                                                     |
| ----------------- | ------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **L1 Foundation** | 1–2           | Psychoeducation (SUD as medical condition), stages of change, craving science, HALT basics                                 | Patient identifies own stage of change correctly                                       |
| **L2 Awareness**  | 3–4           | Trigger mapping, trigger log, high-risk situation identification, pros/cons analysis (decisional balance)                  | Completes trigger map with ≥ 10 entries; identifies top 3 triggers                     |
| **L3 Skills**     | 5–8           | Urge surfing, delay techniques, refusal scripts, escape planning, healthy alternative activities                           | Successfully rides out 3+ urges without using; uses refusal script in vivo             |
| **L4 Deep Work**  | 9–12          | Cognitive restructuring (euphoric recall, rationalization), values clarification, repair relationships, emotional sobriety | Identifies 3 cognitive distortions in real time; writes values statement               |
| **L5 Mastery**    | 13–16         | Relapse prevention plan, sponsor/peer connection, lifestyle redesign, maintenance rituals, chain analysis if lapse         | Has written 1-year maintenance plan; demonstrates chain analysis on hypothetical lapse |

### In-the-Moment Coaching Scripts (3-Step Crisis)

**Craving Script**

1. **HALT** → "Hungry? Angry? Lonely? Tired? Check all four. Fix what you can right now."
2. **SURF** → "This craving is a wave. It will peak in 20–30 minutes. You don't have to act on it. Breathe through it."
3. **DO** → "Call your sponsor. Leave your house. Go to a meeting. One action breaks the loop."

**Social Pressure Script**

1. **PAUSE** → "You don't have to decide in 3 seconds. Take a breath."
2. **SCRIPT** → "Say: 'No thanks, I'm not drinking/using tonight.' You don't owe an explanation."
3. **EXIT** → "If they push, leave. Your recovery matters more than this interaction. Text your sponsor now."

**Post-Lapse Script**

1. **STOP** → "One use is not a relapse. Do not use again today."
2. **CALL** → "Text or call your sponsor/therapist right now. Do not isolate."
3. **LEARN** → "What triggered this? What will you do differently next time? Write it in the app."

### Safety Escalation Protocols

| Trigger                                                           | Immediate Action                                | Escalation Path                                                             |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Patient reports active withdrawal (seizure risk, severe vomiting) | Advise immediate medical care                   | 911 + emergency contact; do not coach through withdrawal                    |
| Overdose suspected (patient unresponsive, blue lips)              | App displays naloxone instructions + 911 prompt | Auto-dial 911 if location services enabled                                  |
| Suicidal ideation + active substance use                          | C-SSRS triggered; block further content         | 988 + therapist alert within 15 minutes                                     |
| Patient reports dealing/manufacturing                             | Note only; no coaching judgment                 | Therapist notification for clinical review (may require mandated reporting) |

### Progress Tracking Metrics

- AUDIT/DAST score (target: drop below cutoff for hazardous use)
- Days sober/clean (continuous counter + rolling 7-day)
- Craving intensity (0–10 daily log; target: average < 4 by week 8)
- Trigger log entries (target: ≥ 5 per week)
- Urge surf success rate (target: ≥ 60% of cravings ridden out)
- Attendance at peer support meetings (self-reported weekly)
- Stage of change progression (target: action by week 6)

### Family/Caregiver Involvement

- **Family psychoeducation** (session 1–2): SUD as brain disease, difference between enabling and supporting
- **Codependency screening**: family members complete brief codependency checklist
- **Family trigger map**: identify household triggers and modify environment
- **Communication skills**: teach "I" statements, boundary-setting with patient
- **Al-Anon/NA family group referral**: strongly encouraged for all family members
- **Family relapse plan**: what to do, who to call, how to respond if patient uses

## 3. EATING DISORDERS

### Therapist Onboarding Checklist

- [ ] Verify specialized ED training or supervision arrangement
- [ ] Complete MAZE ED module certification (4 hours: medical complications, refeeding, FBT basics)
- [ ] Review EDE-Q administration and clinical cutoff scores
- [ ] Confirm medical collaboration pathway (physician, dietitian, psychiatrist)
- [ ] Set up medical stability monitoring protocol (vitals, labs, weight)
- [ ] Review family-based treatment (FBT) principles if treating minors
- [ ] Sign module consent acknowledging medical risk
- [ ] Complete 2 case-based discussions: anorexia medical emergency + binge-purge cycle

### Patient Intake Checklist

- [ ] Administer EDE-Q + SCOFF screening
- [ ] Assess ED subtype (anorexia, bulimia, BED, ARFID, OSFED)
- [ ] Medical stability screen: weight, vitals, orthostatics, menstrual status, electrolytes
- [ ] Assess for medical instability (bradycardia < 50, hypotension, hypothermia, dehydration)
- [ ] Suicide screen (C-SSRS) — ED has elevated suicide risk
- [ ] Self-induced vomiting/laxative/diuretic use frequency and duration
- [ ] Exercise quantity and compulsivity assessment
- [ ] Body checking and weighing behaviors
- [ ] Family history of ED, obesity, anxiety, depression
- [ ] Treatment history and prior level of care

### Session-by-Session Skill Progression (L1–L5)

| Level             | Session Range | Skills Unlocked                                                                                                    | Mastery Checkpoint                                                          |
| ----------------- | ------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **L1 Foundation** | 1–2           | Psychoeducation (ED as coping mechanism), hunger-fullness scale intro, normalization of eating, meal plan basics   | Patient can identify ED thoughts vs. own thoughts                           |
| **L2 Awareness**  | 3–4           | Food rules audit, body checking reduction plan, cognitive restructuring for ED thoughts, meal support intro        | Completes 3-day food + thought log; identifies top 3 food rules             |
| **L3 Skills**     | 5–8           | Exposure to fear foods (hierarchy), response prevention (no purging), mindful eating, distress tolerance for urges | Completes 2 fear food exposures without compensatory behavior               |
| **L4 Deep Work**  | 9–12          | Mirror exposure, values clarification (beyond weight/appearance), body neutrality practice, identity work          | Writes values statement; completes mirror exposure 3x                       |
| **L5 Mastery**    | 13–16         | Relapse prevention, full food flexibility, self-compassion routine, social eating, maintenance meal plan           | Eats socially 2+ times/week without compensatory behavior; has relapse plan |

### In-the-Moment Coaching Scripts (3-Step Crisis)

**Urge to Binge Script**

1. **HALT** → "Hungry? Angry? Lonely? Tired? Rate each 0–10. Address the real need."
2. **DELAY** → "Set a 10-minute timer. During those 10 minutes: drink water, call someone, leave the kitchen."
3. **CHOOSE** → "If you still want to eat after 10 minutes, choose one portion, sit down, eat slowly. No shame either way. Log what happened."

**Urge to Purge Script**

1. **STOP** → "You just ate. That is okay. Your body knows what to do with food."
2. **GROUND** → "Cold water on wrists. 5-4-3-2-1 senses. One slow breath. The urge will pass in 20 minutes."
3. **SUPPORT** → "Text your support person. Or call the crisis line. You don't have to do this alone."

**Body Checking Spiral Script**

1. **NOTICE** → "You're checking your body again. That's the ED talking, not truth."
2. **REDIRECT** → "Put down the mirror/scale. Walk to a different room. Touch something textured."
3. **VALUE** → "What matters to you beyond your body? Call a friend. Write one sentence. Play music."

### Safety Escalation Protocols

| Trigger                                            | Immediate Action                 | Escalation Path                                            |
| -------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| Weight loss > 1 lb/week with BMI < 18              | Flag medical concern             | Immediate physician contact; consider higher level of care |
| Vital signs: HR < 50, BP < 90/60, temp < 95°F      | Advise emergency department      | 911 if syncope, chest pain, severe weakness                |
| Suicidal ideation (C-SSRS positive)                | Crisis plan activation           | 988 + therapist within 15 min + emergency contact          |
| Patient reports purging > 3x/day or blood in vomit | Medical emergency protocol       | 911 + ED referral                                          |
| Refusal to eat for > 24 hours                      | Assess motivation + medical risk | Physician contact; possible inpatient admission            |

### Progress Tracking Metrics

- EDE-Q global score (target: drop below clinical cutoff)
- Weight trend (stabilization or restoration per treatment goal)
- Vital signs log (target: all within safe range)
- Meal plan adherence % (target: ≥ 80%)
- Binge/purge episode frequency (target: 50% reduction by week 6)
- Fear food hierarchy progress (% completed)
- Body checking frequency (target: < 2x/day by week 8)
- Body image flexibility score (target: improvement on validated measure)

### Family/Caregiver Involvement

- **FBT Phase 1** (for minors): parents take control of all meals; app supports parent meal planning + logging
- **Family psychoeducation**: parents learn ED is not a choice, not about vanity, not their fault
- **Siblings support**: age-appropriate explanation; sibling may need own support
- **Family meal**: therapist observes family meal via video or in-person; app provides structure
- **Parent coaching**: how to respond to resistance, secrecy, emotional outbursts at meals
- **Caregiver self-care**: screen parent anxiety/depression; high caregiver distress predicts worse outcomes

## 4. ADHD / EXECUTIVE FUNCTION

### Therapist Onboarding Checklist

- [ ] Verify experience with ADHD assessment and treatment
- [ ] Complete MAZE ADHD module certification (2.5 hours: EF science, coaching model, medication basics)
- [ ] Review ASRS-v1.1 administration and interpretation
- [ ] Confirm understanding of ADHD in adults (particularly women and late-diagnosed)
- [ ] Set up collaboration pathway for medication management (psychiatrist/PCP)
- [ ] Review RSD (rejection sensitive dysphoria) and emotional dysregulation protocols
- [ ] Sign module consent
- [ ] Complete 2 role-plays: task initiation coaching + emotional regulation coaching

### Patient Intake Checklist

- [ ] Administer ASRS-v1.1 Part A + Part B
- [ ] Childhood symptom history (WURS or clinical interview)
- [ ] Screen for comorbidities: anxiety (GAD-7), depression (PHQ-9), substance use (AUDIT), autism traits (AQ-10)
- [ ] Assess executive function domains: working memory, task initiation, time management, emotional regulation, organization
- [ ] Identify current impairments: work, relationships, finances, self-care, driving
- [ ] Medication history: current stimulants/non-stimulants, side effects, adherence
- [ ] Sleep assessment: sleep onset, quality, delayed sleep phase, screen use
- [ ] Strengths inventory: hyperfocus areas, creativity, energy, problem-solving
- [ ] Establish baseline: weekly task completion rate, missed deadlines, financial late fees
- [ ] App onboarding: PWA install, timer setup, notification preferences

### Session-by-Session Skill Progression (L1–L5)

| Level             | Session Range | Skills Unlocked                                                                                                             | Mastery Checkpoint                                                           |
| ----------------- | ------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **L1 Foundation** | 1–2           | Psychoeducation (ADHD as EF difference, not willpower), dopamine basics, time-blindness awareness, self-compassion for ADHD | Patient reframes 3 past "failures" as EF challenges                          |
| **L2 Awareness**  | 3–4           | Externalization systems (calendar, visual cues), body doubling intro, task initiation rituals, environment modification     | Sets up 3 external tools; completes 1 body-doubling session                  |
| **L3 Skills**     | 5–8           | Pomodoro + time-blocking, chunking + implementation intentions, impulse pause strategies, emotional regulation basics       | Completes 5 tasks using Pomodoro; uses impulse pause 3x                      |
| **L4 Deep Work**  | 9–12          | RSD management, DBT-flavored distress tolerance, motivation-by-curiosity, habit stacking, energy management                 | Identifies RSD trigger + applies coping strategy; stacks 2 habits            |
| **L5 Mastery**    | 13–16         | Values-aligned goal architecture, identity-level reframing, long-term system design, maintenance + adaptation               | Has sustainable weekly system; adapts system independently when life changes |

### In-the-Moment Coaching Scripts (3-Step Crisis)

**Task Paralysis Script**

1. **TINY** → "What's the smallest possible first step? Open the email. Write one sentence. Find the file. Do that only."
2. **TIMER** → "Set a 5-minute timer. You only have to work for 5 minutes. Permission to stop after."
3. **BODY DOUBLE** → "Call a friend. Sit on a video call. Tell them what you're doing. Their presence is enough."

**Emotional Dysregulation / RSD Script**

1. **NAME** → "This is rejection-sensitive dysphoria. It's real. It's painful. And it will pass in 20–60 minutes."
2. **PAUSE** → "Do not send the text. Do not quit the job. Do not confront anyone for 1 hour. Set a timer."
3. **SOOTHE** → "Wrap yourself in a blanket. Put on music. Walk outside. Your nervous system needs regulation, not action."

**Impulse Purchase / Risk Script**

1. **WAIT** → "Add to cart. Close the tab. Set a 24-hour timer."
2. **CHECK** → "Ask: Do I need this? Can I afford it? Will I regret this tomorrow? Write the answers."
3. **DECIDE** → "If still unsure after 24 hours, ask your accountability partner. Not alone."

### Safety Escalation Protocols

| Trigger                                                   | Immediate Action                               | Escalation Path                                         |
| --------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Patient reports stimulant misuse or selling medication    | Non-judgmental inquiry; harm-reduction framing | Prescriber notification; possible medication adjustment |
| Driving accidents or near-misses due to inattention       | Safety counseling                              | DMV/medical review referral if pattern                  |
| Impulsive self-harm during emotional dysregulation        | Distress tolerance coaching                    | 988 if active intent; therapist alert within 1 hour     |
| Severe insomnia from stimulants (no sleep 48+ hrs)        | Sleep hygiene coaching; medication review      | Prescriber contact; possible medical evaluation         |
| Financial crisis (eviction, utility shutoff, debt spiral) | Practical problem-solving                      | Social work/resource referral                           |

### Progress Tracking Metrics

- ASRS-v1.1 score (target: drop ≥ 30% by session 12)
- Weekly task completion rate (target: ≥ 70%)
- Missed deadlines count (target: 50% reduction)
- Pomodoro/focus session count per week
- Emotional dysregulation episodes (self-rated 0–10 frequency)
- Sleep quality score (target: improvement on subjective measure)
- Financial management score (late fees, overdrafts, budget adherence)
- Relationship conflict frequency (target: reduction)

### Family/Caregiver Involvement

- **Partner psychoeducation**: ADHD is neurobiological; forgetfulness is not lack of caring
- **Shared system design**: partner helps build external structures (calendar sync, chore charts)
- **Communication coaching**: how to give reminders without nagging; "I need" vs. "You forgot"
- **Parenting with ADHD**: if patient is parent, support for managing children's needs alongside own EF challenges
- **Family ADHD screening**: first-degree relatives have 5x risk; suggest evaluation if symptoms present
- **Accountability partner setup**: designate one family member as weekly check-in partner

## 5. DEPRESSION

### Therapist Onboarding Checklist

- [ ] Verify licensure and depression treatment competency
- [ ] Complete MAZE depression module certification (3 hours: BA therapy, cognitive model, suicide risk assessment)
- [ ] Review PHQ-9 + C-SSRS administration protocols
- [ ] Confirm suicide risk assessment competency and state duty-to-warn requirements
- [ ] Set up 24-hour crisis coverage or backup clinician
- [ ] Review medication collaboration pathway (psychiatrist/PCP)
- [ ] Sign module consent acknowledging suicide-risk responsibilities
- [ ] Complete 2 role-plays: safety planning + behavioral activation session

### Patient Intake Checklist

- [ ] Administer PHQ-9 (item 9 flagged if > 0)
- [ ] If PHQ-9 item 9 > 0: administer full C-SSRS before any other content
- [ ] Assess depression subtype: MDD, persistent, seasonal, postpartum, bipolar screening (mood history)
- [ ] Screen for mania/hypomania (MDQ) to rule out bipolar
- [ ] Medical causes screen: thyroid, B12, vitamin D, anemia, medication side effects
- [ ] Substance use screen (AUDIT/DAST) — alcohol/cannabis can worsen depression
- [ ] Assess functional impairment: work, relationships, self-care, parenting
- [ ] Identify anhedonia severity (key BA target)
- [ ] Social connection inventory: who does patient see/talk to weekly?
- [ ] Sleep assessment: insomnia vs. hypersomnia
- [ ] Establish baseline activity level: hours out of bed, activities per day
- [ ] App onboarding with explicit suicide-care rail explanation

### Session-by-Session Skill Progression (L1–L5)

| Level             | Session Range | Skills Unlocked                                                                                                                                  | Mastery Checkpoint                                                                        |
| ----------------- | ------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **L1 Foundation** | 1–2           | Psychoeducation (depression as withdrawal spiral), activity-mood link, sleep hygiene basics, self-compassion intro                               | Patient explains activity-mood link in own words                                          |
| **L2 Awareness**  | 3–4           | Activity monitoring, pleasure + mastery rating, identify avoided activities, behavioral activation primer                                        | Completes 7-day activity log with ratings                                                 |
| **L3 Skills**     | 5–8           | Activity scheduling (graded task assignment), social reconnection micro-goals, cognitive restructuring (negative triad), rumination interruption | Schedules 3 activities/week; completes 2 thought records; uses rumination interruption 3x |
| **L4 Deep Work**  | 9–12          | Intermediate BA, problem-solving therapy modules, values work, identity after depression, self-criticism → self-compassion                       | Writes values-based activity plan; demonstrates self-compassion response to setback       |
| **L5 Mastery**    | 13–16         | Relapse prevention plan, meaning-making, maintenance activity schedule, long-term goal architecture, peer support connection                     | Has written relapse plan; maintains activity schedule 2+ weeks independently              |

### In-the-Moment Coaching Scripts (3-Step Crisis)

**Suicidal Ideation Script** _(always routes to human)_

1. **STAY** → "You are having thoughts of hurting yourself. Thank you for telling the app. You are not alone."
2. **CONNECT** → "Call or text 988 right now. Someone is there 24/7. Or call your therapist at [number]."
3. **SAFE** → "Remove or secure anything you could use to hurt yourself. Go to a public place. Stay on the phone with 988."

**Can't Get Out of Bed Script**

1. **ONE** → "You don't have to do everything. You only need to do one thing. Sit up. That's it."
2. **BODY** → "Drink water. Eat something. Take a shower. These are victories, not obligations."
3. **CONNECT** → "Text one person: 'Having a hard day.' You don't have to explain. Connection fights isolation."

**Rumination Spiral Script**

1. **NOTICE** → "You're ruminating. Thoughts are looping. This is a depression symptom, not a truth-seeking process."
2. **INTERRUPT** → "Stand up. Walk to a different room. Put on music. Set a 10-minute timer for worrying—after that, stop."
3. **ACT** → "Do one tiny thing from your activity list. One dish. One text. One step outside. Action breaks rumination."

### Safety Escalation Protocols

| Trigger                                                              | Immediate Action                                       | Escalation Path                                                                     |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| PHQ-9 item 9 ≥ 1                                                     | App surfaces C-SSRS; blocks other content              | C-SSRS positive intent/plan → 988 + immediate therapist alert + safety plan display |
| PHQ-9 total ≥ 20                                                     | Flag severe depression                                 | Therapist callback within 4 hours; assess for hospitalization                       |
| Patient reports self-harm (cutting, burning) without suicidal intent | Distress tolerance coaching; wound care info if needed | Therapist alert within 2 hours; assess for escalation                               |
| Sudden mood elevation + decreased sleep + increased activity         | Bipolar screen triggered                               | Therapist alert; MDQ follow-up; possible medication review                          |
| Patient expresses psychotic symptoms (hallucinations, delusions)     | Do not dismiss; assess for safety                      | Immediate psychiatric evaluation; possible 911                                      |

### Progress Tracking Metrics

- PHQ-9 total score (target: drop ≥ 5 points by session 8; remission < 5 by session 16)
- C-SSRS severity tracking (target: sustained zero on intent/plan items)
- Activity log completion rate (target: ≥ 5 entries/week)
- Pleasure + mastery ratings average (target: increase 1+ point by week 6)
- Sleep quality score (target: improvement)
- Social contact frequency (target: ≥ 2 meaningful contacts/week)
- Self-care index (hygiene, meals, exercise; target: ≥ 60% baseline restoration)
- Functional impairment score (target: return to work/social baseline)

### Family/Caregiver Involvement

- **Family psychoeducation**: depression is medical, not weakness; patient's withdrawal is not rejection
- **Safety planning with family**: family knows warning signs, who to call, how to secure means
- **Relationship repair**: coach family on not taking symptoms personally; gentle invitation vs. demand
- **Caregiver burden screen**: family members at risk for burnout; provide resources
- **Parenting support**: if patient is parent, support for maintaining connection with children
- **Activity inclusion**: family joins scheduled activities (walks, meals, games) without pressure

## 6. TRAUMA / PTSD

### Therapist Onboarding Checklist

- [ ] Verify trauma-informed training (TF-CBT, CPT, PE, EMDR, or equivalent)
- [ ] Complete MAZE trauma module certification (4 hours: trauma neuroscience, stabilization, exposure ethics)
- [ ] Review PCL-5 administration and scoring
- [ ] Confirm competency in dissociation screening and management
- [ ] Set up crisis protocol for flashbacks, dissociation, and self-harm
- [ ] Review vicarious trauma self-care plan
- [ ] Sign module consent acknowledging complex trauma scope
- [ ] Complete 2 role-plays: grounding through dissociation + trauma narrative support

### Patient Intake Checklist

- [ ] Administer PCL-5 + Life Events Checklist
- [ ] Assess trauma type: single-event, chronic, complex, developmental, interpersonal
- [ ] Screen for dissociation (DES-II brief) — determines if processing phase is safe
- [ ] Assess window of tolerance: hyperarousal vs. hypoarousal dominance
- [ ] Suicide screen (C-SSRS) — PTSD has elevated suicide risk
- [ ] Substance use screen — common trauma coping mechanism
- [ ] Medical history: chronic pain, autoimmune, GI issues (somatic trauma markers)
- [ ] Assess attachment style and current relationships
- [ ] Identify triggers: sensory, situational, emotional, relational
- [ ] Establish stabilization baseline: can patient self-regulate within 20 minutes?
- [ ] Set therapy goals: patient-defined, not trauma-processing-only
- [ ] App onboarding with grounding tools pre-loaded

### Session-by-Session Skill Progression (L1–L5)

| Level                | Session Range | Skills Unlocked                                                                                                             | Mastery Checkpoint                                                       |
| -------------------- | ------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **L1 Foundation**    | 1–3           | Psychoeducation (trauma brain: amygdala, hippocampus, PFC), window of tolerance, fight-flight-freeze-fawn, polyvagal basics | Patient identifies own dominant arousal state                            |
| **L2 Stabilization** | 4–6           | Grounding (5-4-3-2-1, physical anchor), self-soothing (5 senses), containment, TIPP skills, trigger identification          | Can self-regulate from hyper/hypoarousal within 15 minutes independently |
| **L3 Skills**        | 7–10          | Cognitive restructuring for stuck points, emotion regulation, boundaries, sleep hygiene, body scan                          | Identifies 3 stuck points; completes body scan 3x                        |
| **L4 Processing**    | 11–14         | Trauma narrative (written or imaginal), exposure to avoided situations, meaning-making, impact statement (CPT)              | Completes trauma narrative; SUDS during narrative drops 20+ points       |
| **L5 Integration**   | 15–18         | Reintegration, post-traumatic growth, identity after trauma, relapse prevention, values-based future                        | Writes post-traumatic growth statement; has relapse plan                 |

### In-the-Moment Coaching Scripts (3-Step Crisis)

**Flashback Script**

1. **NOW** → "This is a flashback. You are remembering danger, but you are not in danger right now. Look around. Name today's date."
2. **GROUND** → "Feel your feet on the floor. Hold something cold. Press your back against a wall. You are here."
3. **BREATHE** → "Breathe in 4, hold 4, out 6. Repeat. The flashback will pass in a few minutes. You are safe."

**Dissociation / Shutdown Script**

1. **NOTICE** → "You might be dissociating. That's your brain protecting you. It's okay."
2. **SENSE** → "Name 3 things you see. Wiggle your toes. Drink cold water. Splash your face."
3. **MOVE** → "Stand up. Walk to a different room. Movement brings you back. Text your safe person."

**Nightmare / Sleep Terror Script**

1. **WAKE** → "You had a nightmare. It's over. You are in your bed. Turn on a light."
2. **GROUND** → "Feel the blanket. Look at a familiar object. Breathe slowly."
3. **SOOTHE** → "Listen to calming music. Read something familiar. Don't go back to sleep immediately if you're still activated. Log it."

### Safety Escalation Protocols

| Trigger                                                      | Immediate Action       | Escalation Path                                                                          |
| ------------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Dissociation lasting > 1 hour with functional impairment     | Grounding coaching     | Therapist callback within 2 hours; assess for hospitalization if unable to care for self |
| Self-harm during flashback (patient hurts self to "wake up") | Wound care + grounding | Therapist alert within 1 hour; assess for safety plan update                             |
| Suicidal ideation during trauma processing (common)          | C-SSRS triggered       | 988 + therapist immediate callback; may pause processing phase                           |
| Substance use to cope with flashbacks                        | Harm-reduction framing | Therapist alert; revisit stabilization skills                                            |
| Patient re-enacting trauma in relationships                  | Flag in dashboard      | Therapist review; possible couples/family session                                        |

### Progress Tracking Metrics

- PCL-5 total score (target: drop ≥ 10 points by session 14)
- Hyperarousal subscale (target: reduction in sleep, irritability, hypervigilance)
- Avoidance subscale (target: increase in avoided activities)
- Dissociation frequency (target: reduction)
- Grounding tool use frequency (target: ≥ 3x/week)
- Sleep quality (target: improvement)
- Window of tolerance self-ratings (target: wider window, fewer extremes)
- Functional impairment (target: return to work/social baseline)

### Family/Caregiver Involvement

- **Family psychoeducation**: trauma responses are survival adaptations, not character flaws
- **Trigger mapping with family**: identify household triggers; modify environment
- **Communication coaching**: how to respond to flashbacks/dissociation (don't grab, do ground)
- **Couples work**: address impact of trauma on intimacy, trust, emotional availability
- **Caregiver self-care**: vicarious trauma risk; encourage own therapy/support
- **Family safety plan**: what to do during flashbacks, who to call, how to create calm environment

### Therapist Onboarding Checklist

- [ ] Review AQ-10 or RAADS-R administration (if diagnostic support role)

- [ ] Sign module consent affirming neurodiversity paradigm
- [ ] Complete 2 case discussions: late-diagnosed adult + autistic burnout

### Patient Intake Checklist

- [ ] Administer AQ-10 + RAADS-R (if undiagnosed seeking assessment)
- [ ] Sensory profile assessment: hypersensitivities and hyposensitivities across all senses
- [ ] Screen for co-occurring conditions: anxiety (GAD-7), depression (PHQ-9), ADHD (ASRS), eating (SCOFF)
- [ ] Assess executive function: planning, organization, task initiation, flexibility
- [ ] Evaluate communication preferences: verbal, written, AAC, gestalt language
- [ ] Identify special interests and strengths
- [ ] Assess masking/camouflaging level and burnout history
- [ ] Sleep assessment (common autistic sleep differences)
- [ ] Social connection inventory: quality over quantity
- [ ] Employment/academic accommodations currently in place
- [ ] Identify goals: self-advocacy, unmasking, executive function, social navigation
- [ ] App onboarding with sensory-friendly settings (dark mode, reduced motion, no sound autoplay)

### Session-by-Session Skill Progression (L1–L5)

| Level             | Session Range | Skills Unlocked                                                                                                                               | Mastery Checkpoint                                                |
| ----------------- | ------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **L1 Foundation** | 1–2           | Psychoeducation (autism as neurotype, not deficit), sensory profile understanding, stimming as regulation, self-advocacy intro                | Patient identifies 5 strengths + 3 support needs                  |
| **L2 Awareness**  | 3–4           | Masking recognition, unmasking micro-steps, energy accounting (spoons), routine design, sensory diet                                          | Completes weekly energy log; designs one sensory accommodation    |
| **L3 Skills**     | 5–8           | Executive function tools (visual schedules, timers, chunking), social navigation (not social skills), communication scripts, boundary-setting | Implements 2 EF tools; uses communication script in vivo          |

### In-the-Moment Coaching Scripts (3-Step Crisis)

**Sensory Overload / Meltdown Script**

1. **REDUCE** → "Turn off lights. Cover ears. Leave the room. Reduce input however you can."
2. **STIM** → "Rock, flap, hum, squeeze. Your body needs this. Do not suppress it."
3. **RECOVER** → "You will need 20–60 minutes to recover. Cancel what you can. Rest. Drink water. This is not failure."

**Autistic Burnout Script**

1. **STOP** → "You are in burnout. Everything feels harder. That's real. You didn't imagine it."
2. **REST** → "Reduce demands. Say no to everything non-essential. Sleep. Stim. Do special interest."
3. **SUPPORT** → "Ask for help. Tell someone: 'I'm burned out and need support.' You don't have to mask through this."

**Social Interaction Overwhelm Script**

1. **EXIT** → "You have permission to leave. You don't need an excuse. 'I need to go' is enough."
2. **RECOVER** → "Find a quiet space. Use your sensory tools. Breathe."
3. **REFRAME** → "You are not 'bad at socializing.' Your brain processes social information differently. Quality over quantity."

### Safety Escalation Protocols

| Trigger                                                        | Immediate Action                               | Escalation Path                                                       |
| -------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Meltdown with self-injury (head-banging, hitting)              | Safety coaching (pad surfaces, remove objects) | If injury occurs → medical care; therapist alert                      |
| Autistic burnout with complete functional collapse             | Rest + demand reduction protocol               | Therapist callback within 24 hours; assess for depression comorbidity |
| Suicidal ideation (elevated in late-diagnosed adults)          | C-SSRS triggered                               | 988 + therapist immediate callback                                    |
| Severe restrictive eating (ARFID with weight loss)             | Nutrition assessment                           | Dietitian + physician referral                                        |
| Patient being pressured into ABA or compliance-based treatment | Advocacy coaching                              | Therapist supports patient/family in seeking affirming alternatives   |

### Progress Tracking Metrics

- Sensory overload frequency (target: reduction through accommodation)
- Masking level self-report (target: reduction if goal is unmasking)
- Energy/spoon log stability (target: fewer crash days)
- Executive function task completion (target: improvement with supports)
- Sleep quality (target: improvement)
- Social connection satisfaction (target: quality relationships maintained)
- Self-advocacy instances (target: increase in requesting accommodations)
- Burnout recovery progress (target: return to baseline functioning)

### Family/Caregiver Involvement

- **Neurodiversity education**: autism is not a tragedy; autistic people have always existed
- **Accommodation over compliance**: stop trying to make autistic person "seem normal"; support their needs
- **Sensory environment**: family modifies home environment (lighting, noise, textures)
- **Communication preferences**: respect patient's communication style; learn AAC if used
- **Sibling support**: siblings need age-appropriate education and own support
- **Unschooling/IEP advocacy**: support parents in educational advocacy
- **Connect to autistic community**: peer support is protective against depression

### In-the-Moment Coaching Scripts (3-Step Crisis)

**Escalating Conflict Script**

1. **STOP** → "Someone is flooded. Heart rate is over 100. Take a 20-minute break. Set a timer."
2. **SOOTHE** → "During the break: no rumination, no rehearsal. Breathe. Walk. Listen to music. Come back calm."
3. **REPAIR** → "Return. Say: 'I want to understand your perspective.' Listen first. Speak second."

**Withdrawer Shutdown Script**

1. **NOTICE** → "Your partner has shut down. This is not abandonment—this is overwhelm."
2. **GIVE SPACE** → "Do not pursue. Say: 'I see you're overwhelmed. I'll be here when you're ready.' Leave the room."
3. **RECONNECT** → "When they return, ask: 'Are you ready to talk?' If not, try again later. No punishment for needing space."

**Betrayal / Trust Rupture Script**

1. **PAUSE** → "This pain is real. Do not make big decisions in the first 48 hours."
2. **CONTAIN** → "If you need to talk, set a time limit (20 minutes). Use 'I' statements only. No contempt."
3. **SUPPORT** → "Consider couples therapy. This is not something to fix alone. Book a session."
